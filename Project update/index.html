Computer engineering is a branch of engineering that focuses on the design and maintenance of computer systems, including both hardware and software. It combines the knowledge and skills of electrical engineering and computer science to develop the best computer systems for use in a variety of industries. Computer engineers may design and develop computer systems, create new computer software, develop and maintain networks, and develop and maintain computer security systems. They may also work with networks and computer systems to ensure that they are functioning properly and securely. Computer engineers are responsible for developing and maintaining the hardware, software, and networks that enable the world to communicate and share information.

Mechanical engineering is a branch of engineering that focuses on the design, production, and operation of machinery. It applies the principles of engineering, physics, and materials science for the design, analysis, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems. It encompasses a wide range of topics including mechanics, kinematics, thermodynamics, materials science, structural analysis, and electricity. Mechanical engineers use the core principles as well as other knowledge in the field to design and analyze manufacturing plants, industrial equipment and machinery, heating and cooling systems, motor vehicles, aircraft, watercraft, robotics, medical devices, weapons, and others.

"Powering the Future with Electrical Engineering"

Electrical engineering is a field of engineering that deals with the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. This field covers a wide range of sub-disciplines including power, controls, telecommunications, signal processing, instrumentation, and electronics. Electrical engineers are responsible for designing, developing, testing, and supervising the manufacture of electrical equipment, such as motors, navigation systems, radars, and control systems. They also work on projects that involve electrical power, such as the design of power grids, power transformers, and other power distribution systems. Some electrical engineers specialize in areas such as renewable energy, green energy, or smart grids. Electrical engineers may also be involved in research and development, system analysis, and computer programming.
